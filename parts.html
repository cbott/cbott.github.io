Groups Leading The Movement

Several groups led the way for Native Americans to gain more rights in the late 1900's

1944 NCAI - National Congress of American Indians
1966 AIM - American Indian Movement
1969 NIEA - National Indian Education Association

These groups aimed to raise media awareness for the cause and to force the government to address their issues.

A march led by the AIM. A banner showing "500 years of resistance" is seen with the AIM logo

Demonstrations

Alcatraz

One of the most widely known demonstrations of the movement was the Indian occupation of Alcatraz island. The government had stopped using the island, so according to a treaty from 1868, the land should be returned to the native people. So in 1969, Native Americans occupied the island. The occupation was also seen by many as a comparison between the living conditions at the former prison, and the poor conditions on Indian reservations.

A water tower on Alcatraz displays a similar message

Examples of the Indian Occupation can still be seen today, like the wall of this building on Alcatraz island.

The Trail of Broken Treaties

A picture of the "Trail of Broken Treaties" march, which was organized by the AIM, along with other Native American Rights groups

The Trail of Broken Treaties was another significant event. Native Americans travelled across the country in the fall of 1972 to protest in Washington D.C., this event was partially sponsored by the AIM. The goals of the march were to raise awareness for the struggle faced by Native Americans and to restore former treaties. In the end, the government agreed to complete further treaty negotiations.

Wounded Knee

Another major protest occurred in 1973, again led by the AIM. This time, almost 200 armed Native Americans took over the town of Wounded Knee. The town was the site of a massacre on Native Americans that occurred 83 years earlier. Though the event attracted lots of media coverage, little was actually gained from the excessive violence.

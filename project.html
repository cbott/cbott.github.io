Overview

The Native American civil rights movement began in the late 1960's and went into the 1970's, though Native American nationalism had been growing since the 40's. American Indians hoped to gain back land from the U.S. that they had previously owned and to gain cultural freedom.

Causes and goals

Native Americans had been oppressed for hundreds of years, but the 60's were an important time for a number of reasons. Native Americans were following in the footsteps of the African American movement, but they also had a number of social issues requiring change. Along with having 10 times the average unemployment rate, Native American reservations often had very poor conditions. Historically a lot of unhappiness was caused by the large number of treaties which the U.S. government had ignored.
